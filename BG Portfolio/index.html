About Me

Why & how did I get to where I am today with code? During my breaks in college I worked in retail to have spending money while I was away at college and to go out with friends when I had the time. Sadly, at the end of each break I would count down the days until my last day came for that season at my retail job and it being over was always a huge relief. I majored in something I knew I would love doing as a career for the rest of my life so I wouldn't have these feelings of waiting for the last day of work to come.
Graduation comes and goes, I receive my Bachelors Degree in Digital Design and here I am currently working in a retail job, but this time I am not looking forward to my last day like the other times. Instead, I worked my way into a leadership role and found a passion for helping others grow and leading a team into success. I told myself that this is still not my vision for the rest of my life. I want to be involved with an industry that shapes the world, impacts the lives of others in positive ways and inspires others to do the same.
Being fascinated with how technology is growing and the impact it has on our everyday lives, I started to look into online computer science courses which were about teaching you the theory behind computer science. I felt like I was not getting exactly what I wanted from the courses. My brother and I spoke (who has a Bachelor of Science degree) and he told me to learn something called HTML and CSS because you could instantly see what you are creating and working on. This has now turned into a never ending "I want more" process and I find myself spending several hours a day constantly learning. My first month passes by and I uploaded my first website written in HTML and CSS from scratch and I felt like I had just taken over the world. Was it the best website ever created? Nowhere near but it was a step in the right direction of what is turning into a passion of mine. Along comes JavaScript and my progress slowed down a bit due to this overwhelming amount of information being thrown at me from different sources such as udemy courses, teamtreehouse, stackoverflow, etc. After grasping the basics of JavaScript, a little bit of jQuery , and familiarzing myself with Bootstrap, I had then realized, this is it! I have found what I love to do, and I am spending countless hours learning more and more, day by day, and building this better version of myself. Each day is becoming more addicting than the day before.
Moving forward now a couple months, and I completely re worked my first website, learned how to make it responsive to different screen sizes, and learned how to create a simpler UI, I am now taking more steps on this endless path of learning Front End Web Development and could not be more excited on what the world of code has to offer me.
